# Telestrations: A Party Drawing-and-Guessing Game

## Executive Summary  
Telestrations is a fast-paced party game blending Pictionary with “telephone.”  Introduced in 2009 by USAopoly (now The Op Games)【39†L73-L76】, it won BoardGameGeek’s “Best Party Game” of 2010【29†L174-L175】.  Players sketch a secret word and pass sketchbooks around, alternating drawing and guessing.  Laughter ensues as drawings are misinterpreted over several passes.  The game supports 4–12 players (depending on edition) in 3-round matches【39†L73-L76】【44†L52-L60】.  Components typically include sketchbooks, erasable markers, and cards of prompts; later editions and variants (After Dark adult version, Upside Drawn team edition, themed expansions) add more cards and books【29†L181-L184】【39†L73-L76】.  Official turn sequence: each player writes a chosen prompt, then 60 seconds to draw it (page 1), pass the book, the next player guesses (page 2), and so on until books return.  Then players reveal the full drawing/guess chains and optionally score points (favorite sketch, favorite guess, matching final guess)【39†L29-L37】【36†L177-L185】.  House rules abound (e.g. no scoring for pure fun).  Telestrations is ideal for mixed-age casual groups who enjoy creativity and humor.  The guide below details setup, gameplay, scoring, common variants, etiquette and strategy, plus illustrative examples of rounds (with images) and a flowchart of the turn cycle.

## Game Overview and History  
Telestrations was first released in 2009 by USAopoly (The Op Games) as a “sketching version” of the classic telephone game【29†L137-L139】.  It gained rapid popularity among casual/family gamers, winning BGG’s 2010 Best Party Game【29†L174-L175】.  Since then the line has grown: after the original 8-player edition, USAopoly released a 12-player “Party Pack” (2013)【29†L181-L184】, a family-friendly 4–6 player box (2012), and an adult-themed “After Dark” edition (2015) for mature groups【29†L181-L184】【69†L5-L8】.  Recent variants include a team-based “Upside Drawn” (2020) and 80s/90s themed prompt expansions【29†L181-L184】.  All editions share the core draw-and-pass mechanic; they differ mainly in component counts and card lists.  (Publisher descriptions: e.g. *6P 2nd Edition – 4–6 players, 65 cards, 6 sketchbooks*【43†L521-L526】【44†L55-L63】; *12P Party Pack – 4–12 players, 160 cards, 12 books*【38†L556-L560】【39†L47-L55】.)  The artwork and materials have evolved (colorful sketchbooks, markers, prompt cards), but rules remain straightforward.

## Components and Setup  
Each player needs a dry-erase sketchbook and marker of a unique color, and all use a common deck of prompt cards.  (Typical contents for 6P: 65 double-sided cards with ~130 prompts, 6 sketchbooks, 6 markers【43†L562-L569】【44†L55-L63】.  The 8P box has ~130 cards and 8 books; the 12P box 160 cards and 12 books【38†L556-L560】【39†L47-L55】.)  Setup is simple:
- **Distribute Materials:** Give each player an erasable sketchbook and matching dry-erase marker【39†L27-L35】.  Place all prompt cards face-down in a central stack.  
- **Write Names:** Players write their name on the front cover of their book (often the first page) and note the coil color【39†L46-L54】.  This helps track books as they circulate.  
- **Draw Cards:** Depending on variant, either (A) each player draws one card from the deck and chooses one prompt on it, or (B) use a die-roll: all players draw a card and one person rolls a die to pick which numbered word on the cards to use【41†L119-L127】.  (Second-edition rules favor option A: “Each player draws a card and selects one of the prompts… secretly write it in your book. Don’t say it out loud!”【41†L119-L127】.)  
- **Select Prompt:** With the chosen word or phrase written in secrecy on page 1 of their own book, each player is ready to start Round 1【41†L119-L127】.  
- **Timer:** Prepare the 60-second timer (built into the box or a phone timer). The game is played in 3 rounds; after each round, erase and draw new cards. (The turn flow itself is the same each round.)  

  <small>※ *Tip:* Ensure all players understand that after each sketch or guess they must flip forward a page and wait for everyone before passing【36†L154-L158】. Simultaneous passing prevents confusion.  

## Turn Sequence (Play-by-Play)  
Each round proceeds simultaneously for all players in these phases:

1. **Write Secret (if using a die-roll):** If a die is used, one player rolls now to pick which prompt number to use【36†L145-L149】.  Otherwise, skip.  
2. **Sketching (60 seconds):** Everyone draws the secret prompt on page 1 of their sketchbook.  Start the timer (60s).  Players should not reveal words or letters — no text in the drawings【41†L158-L162】.  Sketch quickly and as clearly as you can.  When time is up, have a final second to finish.  
3. **Pass to Left:** Once drawing is done, each player flips the book to page 2 (hiding the sketch) and passes the open book to the player on their left【36†L112-L119】.  
4. **Guessing (60 seconds):** Now each player (having received a book) secretly looks at the sketch on page 1, then writes their best guess for it on page 2.  Again use the timer (60s).  Hints: don’t leave the page blank or just write “?”【36†L149-L152】; write any guess that comes to mind.  When done, flip to page 3 and pass the book left.  
5. **Repeat:** Players alternate drawing and guessing with a fresh timer each time: draw on odd pages, guess on even pages (pages 3,4,5,…).  After each action, flip page and pass. Continue until all books are returned to their original owners.  (With *even* number of players you draw first then guess, so you end on a guess; if *odd* players, a special “initial pass” step is done so the final page is still a guess【41†L131-L139】【41†L204-L212】.)  

6. **Reveal & Score:** Once the original book returns, put down markers. Each player takes turns flipping through their own book aloud, showing the chain of sketch→guess→sketch→…【36†L135-L142】.  The group reacts and laughs at how the prompt evolved. Optionally, use the official scoring (next section) or skip scoring entirely. Then prepare for the next round (erase all pages, draw new prompts).  

A mermaid flowchart of these steps is shown below (start round, write word, sketch, guess, repeat loop, reveal, score, next round):

```mermaid

    Setup[Setup: get book, marker, card] --> Write[Write prompt (if using die)]
```

## Scoring and Variants  
**Official Scoring (Competitive vs Friendly):** Telestrations can be played *casually* or with scoring.  The official rulebook provides two scoring modes【39†L29-L37】.  A typical 3-round game uses **Friendly Scoring**: each player awards – in their own book – 1 point to whichever *sketch* they liked best, and 1 point to whichever *guess* they liked best.  Also, if the final guess in your book matches your original word exactly, you give yourself 1 point【39†L29-L37】.  (You mark points on a sheet in the sketchbook.)  In **Competitive Scoring**, all players score on everyone’s reveals【39†L37-L45】: any player who made a guess matching the prior player’s drawing gets 1 point, and any player whose drawing helped cause a correct guess gets 1 point.  In either mode, the player with most points after 3 rounds wins.  

**Common House Rules:** Many groups simplify scoring or skip it entirely, since the laughter is the main reward.  Some popular variants:
- **No scoring:** Simply play each round for fun, not points. After everyone reveals, just say “great job!” and proceed.  
- **Alternate passing:** Reverse the pass direction each round for variety (rulebook suggestion【39†L96-L100】).  
- **Limited prompts:** Use only certain categories (e.g. animals, movies) or custom words suited to the group.  
- **Time adjustments:** For very young players or large boards, extend the timer beyond 60s.  
- **Upside Drawn rule (team play):** In the Upside Drawn spin-off, teams alternate between people drawing two sketches side-by-side, but the core draw-guess-pass loop is similar【71†L1-L4】.

## Strategy and Etiquette Tips  
- **Keep it simple:** Focus on the most recognizable feature of the prompt.  You only have 60 seconds and a small drawing space, so avoid over-detail. (As the rulebook notes: *“The worse a drawer you are, the funnier it can get!”*【41†L158-L162】.)  
- **Use symbols and context:** Even though letters/numbers aren’t allowed in sketches【41†L158-L162】, you can draw symbolic cues (e.g. hearts for love, musical notes for songs) and rely on shared context.  
- **Guess boldly:** Don’t be shy about writing a strange guess.  The hints advise: *“Don’t just put a question mark or leave blank. Write any guess… no matter how silly.”*【41†L163-L169】  
- **Pass properly:** Always flip to the *next* blank page before passing, so sketches and guesses aren’t erased accidentally.  All players should pass at once on cue【36†L154-L158】.  This keeps the chain in order.  
- **Stay quiet:** Keep your word and guess secret. No peeking or comments until the reveal.  (Laughing can be contagious, but don’t give away your guess.)  
- **Erase cleanly:** Between rounds, erase all pages completely.  Ink can ghost if not fully wiped【39†L79-L85】.  
- **Be gracious:** The spirit is lighthearted. Praise funny drawings and guesses (“Wow, I never would have thought of that!”). Reward creativity when scoring (“That was my favorite sketch!”). Avoid criticism of poor art – it’s part of the fun!  

## Common Mistakes & Troubleshooting  
- **Book mis-passing:** If someone forgets to pass or passes the wrong book, use the coil colors/names to sort it out before proceeding.  It helps to double-check your sketchbook color before drawing【39†L46-L54】.  
- **Timer hiccups:** If the timer runs out mid-draw, let players finish the current stroke. Don’t start passing with the timer still on.  
- **Blank pages:** Occasionally a player may skip making a guess or drawing. If a page is blank, just pass and continue; on reveal you’ll laugh at the missing link. (Encourage at least a wild guess rather than nothing.)  
- **Marker dried out:** If a marker dries, any dry-erase marker works as a substitute【39†L79-L85】.  Carry extras for large games.  
- **Rules confusion:** Clarify at start whether you use the die-roll or category-option for prompts【41†L119-L127】, and how many rounds you’ll play.  If uncertain about passing order, recall the goal: final page should always be a *guess*, not a sketch【41†L204-L212】.  
- **Keeping score:** Record points on the score sheet after each reveal. Check that everyone applied the friendly/competitive rules correctly (these differ!).  If playing casually, simply say “you won!” as soon as the round ends【36†L159-L167】.  

## Recommended Player Counts and Group Types  
Telestrations scales well with group size.  The 6-player box is designed for **4–6 players【43†L521-L526】**; the 8-player for **up to 8** (4–8)【44†L50-L58】; the 12-player Party Pack supports **4–12 players【38†L556-L560】**.  Note that with more players, each round has more passes (longer chain) and takes slightly longer.  Smaller groups can use the bigger box, but with fewer people involved per round.  

Ideal groups are mixed-age family or friends who enjoy humor and casual creativity.  Age 10+ is recommended (younger kids might enjoy it if words are easy).  It’s great for family game night, parties, or ice-breakers.  Competitive gamers may enjoy the twist of competitive scoring, but Telestrations shines as a lighthearted party game where even poor drawing is celebrated.  For a very large crowd, consider splitting into teams or multiple games simultaneously.
